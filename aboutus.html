🏡 Who We Are

A warm Islamic Montessori home away from home

Our school was founded with a simple goal: to give young children a place where they can grow with strong Islamic values while learning through a thoughtful, child-centered Montessori education.

We believe the early years should be filled with wonder, beauty, structure, and love. That is why our classrooms are designed to be calm, inviting, and purposeful, helping children build independence, confidence, respect, and a true love of learning.

Every lesson, routine, and interaction is meant to support the whole child so they can blossom academically, socially, emotionally, and spiritually.

🌱 Rooted in Fitrah

We honor the natural goodness, curiosity, and dignity Allah placed within every child.

🧩 Hands-On Discovery

Children learn by touching, exploring, trying, repeating, and discovering with joy.

💛 Gentle Guidance

We nurture adab, responsibility, and independence with warmth, consistency, and encouragement.

Faith

Islam is not treated like an add-on. It shapes the atmosphere, language, and character of our school day.

Care

We work to make every child feel seen, safe, and valued so they can confidently engage with the world.

Growth

Children are encouraged to do for themselves, think for themselves, and take pride in their progress.

Joy

We believe learning should feel beautiful, meaningful, and exciting throughout the preschool years.

🧕 Leadership

Meet Sister Fatin Kheirallah, MSW

Fatin Kheirallah, MSW, has served as a dedicated educator within the community for over 25 years. Throughout her career, Sister Fatin has devoted herself to studying and implementing a variety of educational approaches aimed at unlocking the unique potential with which every child has been blessed. Her lifelong mission has been to help cultivate young, healthy Muslim minds who, by the will of Allah, will grow to become confident leaders of the Ummah.

At the heart of her philosophy is the belief that children thrive when they are encouraged to think independently, take responsibility from an early age, and develop strong intellectual and cognitive reasoning skills. She has always been deeply committed to nurturing not only academic growth, but also character, confidence, and a sense of purpose.

After extensive study and years of experience, Sister Fatin found that the Montessori method offered the most effective and developmentally sound approach for early childhood education. Its emphasis on independence, self-discipline, hands-on learning, and respect for each child’s natural development aligns beautifully with Islamic values.

By integrating the Montessori philosophy with Islamic values and the Nurtured Heart Approach, Sister Fatin has helped develop an environment in which children truly flourish. Under her guidance, children blossom into strong, independent thinkers with Islam deeply rooted in their hearts. With every step, they grow in confidence; with every activity, they find joy; and with every lesson, their love for learning continues to deepen.
